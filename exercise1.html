The Slow Art of Paying Attention in a Distracted World

There's a particular kind of exhaustion that comes not from doing too much, but from thinking too shallowly. We skim headlines, scroll feeds, and mistake the sensation of being informed for actually understanding something.

Why Slowness Matters Now

Writers have always known this. Good prose is not merely about stringing sentences together — it's about forcing the reader to stop, reconsider, sit with an idea long enough that it becomes their own.

"Attention is the rarest and purest form of generosity." — Simone Weil

What Good Reading Looks Like

  • Resisting the urge to skip to the conclusion
  • Pausing on sentences that feel uncomfortable
  • Returning to paragraphs you didn't fully understand
  • Keeping a notebook beside you, even if you never open it

A Simple Practice — In Order

  1. Find a piece of writing that demands something from you
  2. Read it once quickly, then once more slowly
  3. Write a single sentence about what changed between the two reads
  4. Come back to it in a week and see what you missed
